# Hume AI

## Snapshot

- Category: Voice AI infrastructure
- Company: Hume AI
- Website: https://www.hume.ai/
- Status: Active API/platform
- Last researched: 2026-06-21

## Product Thesis

Hume's thesis is that expressive, empathic voice is an infrastructure layer for more natural AI interactions across apps, not necessarily a standalone companion.

## Core Interaction Loop

Developers integrate Hume's Empathic Voice Interface or TTS APIs into voice-first agents, phone agents, tutors, companions, or assistants.

## Job-To-Be-Done

Give product builders speech models that can interpret and generate expressive, responsive voice interactions.

## Memory Model

Hume is not memory-first. Memory would be supplied by the application using Hume's voice interface.

## Voice, Avatar, and Embodiment

Voice is the product. Hume's EVI and TTS are directly relevant to Companions' voice stack and expressive state without requiring custom speech research.

## Retention Loop

For Hume, retention is developer/platform retention: quality, latency, pricing, and API reliability. For products built on Hume, retention depends on the app layer.

## Pricing and Business Model

Usage-based API pricing with free/pro/business tiers. Public pricing lists EVI and TTS minutes/characters and higher-tier concurrency/support.

## What Users Love

Developers and reviewers value expressive, low-friction voice capabilities that can make agents feel less flat.

## What Users Complain About

Risks include cost at scale, dependency on a vendor, unpredictable emotional/personality outputs, and ethical concerns around simulated empathy.

## Strategic Lessons

Use Hume-like systems as commodity infrastructure. Companions should not spend original research effort recreating expressive speech until the memory/accountability loop works.

## Open Questions

- Does Hume's latency meet real-time companion expectations?
- How controllable is voice style over long sessions?
- What is the real cost per engaged daily user?
