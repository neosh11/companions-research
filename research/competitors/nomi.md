# Nomi

## Snapshot

- Category: AI companion app
- Company: GLIMPSE.AI, INC.
- Website: https://nomi.ai/
- Status: Active consumer product
- Last researched: 2026-06-21

## Product Thesis

Nomi's thesis is that an AI companion should feel persistent, personal, and relationship-like through strong personality, short/medium/long-term memory, and flexible friend or partner framing.

## Core Interaction Loop

Users create a Nomi, chat over time, develop shared history, use memory-backed conversations, and optionally explore voice or roleplay-style interaction.

## Job-To-Be-Done

Provide a persistent AI counterpart that remembers personal details and sustains an ongoing relationship-like thread.

## Memory Model

Memory is central to the product. Official copy emphasizes short, medium, and long-term memory. A company Reddit statement describes broad memory access under one plan, but those exact operational details should be rechecked against current product behavior.

## Voice, Avatar, and Embodiment

Nomi is more memory/personality-led than 3D-led. Visual identity exists through companion presentation and app surfaces, while voice appears as a premium feature in third-party summaries.

## Retention Loop

Retention comes from accumulated memory, companion identity, relationship continuity, and a paid plan that appears to avoid fragmenting core memory into many add-ons.

## Pricing and Business Model

Freemium with paid subscription. App listings confirm in-app purchases; third-party reviews report a subscription around the mid-teens monthly, but current pricing should be verified in-app.

## What Users Love

Positive signals focus on memory, personality consistency, and the feeling that the companion builds context over time.

## What Users Complain About

Common risks are the same as any memory-first companion: memory mistakes, pricing sensitivity, and possible mismatch between relationship framing and actual model behavior.

## Strategic Lessons

Nomi is one of the most relevant benchmarks for Companions. Study how it packages memory as the product, not as a background feature.

## Open Questions

- How transparent are memory edits and deletions?
- Does one-plan pricing reduce trust issues compared with heavy feature gating?
- How does Nomi handle memory conflicts?
