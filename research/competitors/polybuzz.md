# PolyBuzz

## Snapshot

- Category: AI character chat / roleplay app
- Company: PolyBuzz
- Website: https://www.polybuzz.ai/
- Status: Active consumer product
- Last researched: 2026-06-21

## Product Thesis

PolyBuzz's thesis is high-volume free/private/unrestricted character chat with a very large character library and roleplay-first discovery.

## Core Interaction Loop

Users browse characters, start private chats, create characters, roleplay, and upgrade for a more usable or enhanced experience.

## Job-To-Be-Done

Provide quick access to many AI characters for immersive roleplay and fantasy scenarios.

## Memory Model

The FAQ emphasizes private character chat and character creation. App Store reviews mention permanent memory as an added feature, but details and pricing need verification.

## Voice, Avatar, and Embodiment

PolyBuzz is visual-character-led through profiles, categories, and roleplay presentation, but not obviously 3D-bodied.

## Retention Loop

Retention comes from character library size, existing chats, user-created bots, premium subscription, and possibly permanent memory.

## Pricing and Business Model

Freemium with membership/subscription. App reviews discuss premium pricing around $20/month and heavy ad pressure on free use, but current pricing should be checked in-app.

## What Users Love

Users praise roleplay variety, character volume, and value when premium feels reasonably priced.

## What Users Complain About

Complaints include ads, pricing changes, subscription value, and reliability around promised benefits.

## Strategic Lessons

PolyBuzz suggests memory can be a paid differentiator even in roleplay apps. Companions should avoid ad-heavy retention if trust is the product.

## Open Questions

- What exactly is "permanent memory" in PolyBuzz?
- How private are chats in practice?
- Does ad pressure drive conversion or churn?
