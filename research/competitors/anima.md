# Anima

## Snapshot

- Category: AI friend / companion app
- Company: Apperry Ltd. / Anima
- Website: https://myanima.ai/
- Status: Active consumer product
- Last researched: 2026-06-21

## Product Thesis

Anima positions itself as a simple AI friend and companion available for quick chats, with personality customization and paid relationship/roleplay expansion.

## Core Interaction Loop

Users chat with an AI friend, personalize the companion, and upgrade for expanded modes, customization, and deeper interaction.

## Job-To-Be-Done

Provide lightweight AI companionship and casual conversation without requiring deep setup.

## Memory Model

Anima marketing and third-party summaries mention memory-based conversations, but detailed user controls and memory quality are unclear.

## Voice, Avatar, and Embodiment

Anima uses an avatar-style app presentation and customization, but its core appears to be chat-first rather than 3D-first.

## Retention Loop

Retention comes from basic availability, personality customization, and paid relationship/roleplay features.

## Pricing and Business Model

Freemium with premium upgrade. Mozilla's privacy guide and third-party pricing reviews describe paid relationship/status features around the low double-digit monthly range, but current in-app pricing should be verified.

## What Users Love

Positive signals are simplicity, approachable onboarding, and casual companion access.

## What Users Complain About

Complaints include limited depth, privacy concerns in Mozilla's review category, pricing confusion, and unclear value versus stronger memory-first competitors.

## Strategic Lessons

Anima shows the danger of being a generic AI friend. Companions should avoid light chat as the main wedge and compete on persistent memory, plans, and avatar presence.

## Open Questions

- What does Anima actually remember and expose?
- Which paid features create durable retention?
- How does Anima handle privacy-sensitive companion data?
