# Pi / Inflection

## Snapshot

- Category: Personal AI assistant / companion
- Company: Inflection AI
- Website: https://hey.pi.ai/
- Status: Active app listing; company focus shifted toward enterprise after 2024 leadership changes
- Last researched: 2026-06-21

## Product Thesis

Pi's thesis is polished, human-centered conversation: a personal AI that helps users think through decisions, brainstorm, get inspired, and talk in a warmer voice than a generic assistant.

## Core Interaction Loop

Users chat with Pi for advice, planning, brainstorming, and casual conversation. The app listing emphasizes that Pi evolves with more use.

## Job-To-Be-Done

Be a thoughtful conversational assistant for decision-making, ideation, and everyday thinking.

## Memory Model

Google Play copy says Pi grows with the user and understands them better over time. Public detail on memory controls and retention is limited.

## Voice, Avatar, and Embodiment

Pi is primarily a voice/text personality product, not an avatar product. Its lesson is tonal polish and conversational flow rather than visual embodiment.

## Retention Loop

Retention depended on conversational quality, personal tone, and habit formation around thinking with Pi. Market history suggests this was not enough to protect the consumer strategy from cost and strategic pressure.

## Pricing and Business Model

Pi has been consumer-facing, but Inflection pivoted toward enterprise/API/on-premise opportunities after Microsoft hired key leaders in March 2024. Axios reported rate limits for Pi in August 2024 during the enterprise pivot.

## What Users Love

Positive signals center on Pi's warmth, clarity, and usefulness for thinking through decisions.

## What Users Complain About

Complaints and market concerns center on uncertain roadmap, rate limits, and the product's position after the Microsoft transition.

## Strategic Lessons

Pi is the cautionary example for "excellent conversation is not enough." Companions needs a sharper job-to-be-done and an economically durable retention loop.

## Open Questions

- What is Pi's current long-term consumer roadmap?
- How much user data portability exists?
- Which parts of Pi's conversational tone are reproducible with current APIs?
