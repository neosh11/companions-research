# Kuki

## Snapshot

- Category: AI chatbot / virtual influencer
- Company: Iconiq AI / Pandorabots lineage
- Website: https://www.kuki.ai/
- Status: Active web/chatbot presence; consumer role differs by channel
- Last researched: 2026-06-21

## Product Thesis

Kuki's thesis is a personality-led AI character that can operate as chatbot, influencer, and embodied digital persona across channels.

## Core Interaction Loop

Users chat with Kuki through the website or connected platforms, while brands can treat Kuki as a virtual influencer or conversational IP.

## Job-To-Be-Done

For consumers: talk to a recognizable AI persona. For businesses: use a proven chatbot/character brand.

## Memory Model

Kuki is historically conversation/persona-led rather than personal long-term memory-led. Any personal memory depth needs direct product testing.

## Voice, Avatar, and Embodiment

Kuki is important for embodiment history: a chatbot with a visible persona and influencer positioning rather than an invisible assistant.

## Retention Loop

Retention comes from novelty, persona consistency, humor, brand recognition, and platform availability.

## Pricing and Business Model

Consumer chat appears free after account sign-in. Commercial applications appear to be the monetization path, but current enterprise pricing is not public in the basic product surface.

## What Users Love

Kuki has long-standing recognition and awards history. Users and observers value its personality consistency and historical role in social chatbot design.

## What Users Complain About

Likely limitations include older chatbot feel, limited personal memory, and less modern companion depth versus LLM-native apps.

## Strategic Lessons

Kuki is a reminder that a memorable companion needs a stable persona, not just model intelligence. But persona without personal memory may not be enough for Companions.

## Open Questions

- What current model stack powers Kuki?
- How much user-specific memory exists today?
- Is Kuki still relevant to modern consumer companion expectations?
